Mission

Mawlukutimk is a collaborative formed to design in the context of Mi’kmaq culture. Collectively and individually, members of the group have been involved in sustained, in-depth explorations of ways of knowing and doing. This has, at times, involved studying and documenting the work and knowledge of elders, with consideration of how that knowledge currently applies. The notion of Etuaptmumk or Two-Eyed Seeing, as developed and articulated by Elder Dr. Albert Marshall, has deeply informed how we understand tradition and how we approach the application of the principles and understandings from within the tradition to how we design for the future. Etuaptmumk / Two-Eyed Seeing applies to understandings of building science expressed in the knowledge of material, structures, and environmental relationships that are demonstrated and embedded in traditional Mi’kmaq practices. The principles learned have then been studied in terms of how they can be adapted to the requirements of contemporary practice, together with a consideration of their seven-generation effects. Each of the members of Mawlukutimk have studied Indigenous ways of knowing, making and building in consideration of their contemporary and future relevance, as well as their historic importance.The purpose of this body of work has always been to foster cultural renewal and continuity, not only in terms of expressions of culture, but an understanding of the principles underlying those cultural expressions. In doing their work, members of the group have a long history of consultation with Mi’kmaq of all communities and generations, particularly the elders, for whom we give thanks, Wela’lin.
